NZ Police Vehicle Theft Analysis

View Dashboard

The Problem

Identifying patterns in criminal activity is essential for resource allocation. This project explores a 6-month dataset from the New Zealand Police Department to uncover high-risk theft profiles and hotspots.

The Data

Source: NZ Police Dept (via Kaggle).
Attributes: Vehicle type, make, year, color, date stolen, and geographic region.

Methodology

  • SQL Joins & CTEs: Linking theft incidents with granular vehicle attributes.
  • Advanced Aggregations: Calculating frequency across regions and timeframes.
  • Looker Studio: Transforming raw SQL outputs into an executive-level dashboard.

Key Insights

95%

of stolen vehicles were Standard models, while Luxury cars accounted for only 4%.

36%

Total thefts occurred in Auckland, identifying it as the primary hotspot.

M/T

Thefts peaked on Mondays and Tuesdays, suggesting higher vulnerability after weekends.

Top Makes: Toyota (15.7%), Nissan (10.6%).

PISA 2012: Global Student Performance

View Analysis

The Problem

As part of the Udacity Data Analyst Nanodegree, this project investigates the PISA 2012 dataset. The goal was to identify which variables—ranging from gender and geography to teacher practices—most significantly impact the academic success of 15-year-old students globally.

The Data

Volume: 510,000 student records with 636 unique features.
Focus: Socio-economic status, school resources, and regional disparities (OECD vs. Non-OECD).
Download Raw Dataset

Methodology

  • Data Wrangling: Cleaning a massive 600+ column dataset using Pandas.
  • Multivariate Visualization: Using Seaborn and Matplotlib to find correlations.
  • Hypothesis Testing: Statistical validation of performance gaps.

Global Insights

Parental Influence: High parental education levels proved to be the strongest predictor of student success.

Digital Divide: Access to school computers improved scores, yet students who *never* used them scored lowest.

Regional Excellence: Students in East Asia and Western Europe significantly outperformed the global average.
