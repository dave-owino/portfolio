SQL: NZ POLICE THEFT ANALYSIS

Motor Vehicle Theft Analysis

The Problem

This project analyzes motor vehicle theft data from the New Zealand police department and focuses on SQL-based exploration. Using SQL statements, I conduct exploratory data analysis (EDA), perform table joins, and apply aggregations to uncover meaningful patterns. Key findings are visualized in a Google Looker Studio dashboard, aiding law enforcement and policymakers in enhancing community safety.

The Data

Source: NZ Police Dept (Kaggle).
Scope: Six-month dataset including details like vehicle type, make, year, color, date stolen, and region.

Methodology

  • SQL Joins & Subqueries to link theft incidents with vehicle attributes.
  • Aggregations to identify top stolen makes, models, and theft hotspots.
  • Google Looker Studio for final dashboard visualization.

Insights

These findings provide valuable insights into vehicle theft trends.

  • Luxury vs Standard: Luxury vehicles accounted for only 4% of stolen cars, while 95% were standard vehicles.
  • Regions: Auckland had the highest proportion of thefts at 36%, followed by Canterbury at 14.5%.
  • Makes: Toyota (15.7%), Trailer (11.9%), and Nissan (10.6%) were the most targeted.
  • Timing: Thefts peaked on April 4, with Mondays and Tuesdays being the most common days.

PYTHON: PISA STUDENT PERFORMANCE

PISA2012 Analysis

The Problem

Part of the Udacity Nanodegree, this project analyzes the PISA2012 dataset to explore factors influencing the academic performance of 15-year-old students. It investigates school choice, gender, location, and teacher practices on scores.

The Data

Source: PISA2012 Dataset.
Volume: 510,000 students, 636 features.
Download Dataset

Methodology

  • Correlation analysis between socio-economic status and scores.
  • Hypothesis testing on gender performance gaps.
  • Visualization of regional disparities (OECD vs Non-OECD).

Insights

Impact of parental education, gender, and technology access.

  • Parental Education: Higher levels correlate strongly with better scores. Females outperform males at most levels.
  • Socio-Economics: Wealth impacts performance significantly in OECD countries.
  • Tech Access: Access to desktops at school improves performance, but non-users in computer-equipped schools score lowest.
  • Regional: Non-OECD students excelled in East Asia and Western Europe despite lower global averages.

EXCEL: KCSE RESULTS DASHBOARD

KCSE 2023 Analysis

The Problem

Initiated by the sub-county director of education, this task involves analyzing KCSE 2023 results for 19 schools. The analysis compares 2023 results with 2022 and 2021 to identify trends, gender performance gaps, and school rankings.

The Data

Source: Sub-county education records.
Volume: 2,400 students across 19 schools.
Scope: Grades, gender, subjects, and year-over-year comparison.

Methodology

  • Excel Data Modeling using Power Pivot.
  • Advanced formulas for Improvement Index calculation.
  • Interactive Dashboards for stakeholder reporting.

Insights

Grade trends and school performance analysis.

  • Improvement: 13% improvement in students scoring C+ and above in 2023.
  • Decline: 50% increase in students scoring grade E, highlighting a widening achievement gap at bottom.
  • Gender: 15% overall attained C+ or higher. Girls (17%) outperformed Boys (13%).
  • Schools: Ribe Boys, Kombeni Girls, and Ribe Girls topped rankings. Bwagamoyo showed the highest decline.

POWER BI: SPROCKET CENTRAL ANALYTICS

Sprocket Central Marketing

The Problem

The project involves analyzing Sprocket Central Pty Ltd's datasets (demographics, addresses, transactions) to optimize marketing strategies. The goal is to identify trends and high-value customers among 1,000 new prospects to drive business growth.

The Data

Source: Sprocket Central Pty Ltd.
Tables: Customer Demographics, Addresses, 3 Months Transactions.

Methodology

  • Feature Engineering to create customer segments.
  • Predictive modeling for purchase likelihood.

Customer demographics and purchasing behavior.

SQL: AIRLINES SALES & OPERATIONS

Airlines Insights

The Problem

Analysis of a Kaggle SQLite dataset to uncover revenue drivers, seasonal trends, and delay patterns. Built interactive dashboards to communicate operational insights clearly.

The Data

Source: Kaggle Airlines Dataset.
Scope: Ticket sales, flight schedules, delay codes, airport info.

Methodology

  • Complex SQL queries using CTEs and Window Functions.
  • Python (Pandas) for initial EDA.
  • Looker Studio for final visualization.

Insights

Revenue and operational efficiency findings.

  • Airports: Domodedovo (1,986M), Sheremetyevo (1,579M), Tolmachevo (799M) are top revenue hubs.
  • Seasonality: July (63%) and August (34%) had highest sales.
  • Delays: Ulyanovsk East Airport had most delays. Flights to Moscow City and Kazan City experienced >3hr delays.
